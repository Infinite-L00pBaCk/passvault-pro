Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about PassVault Pro, from encryption mechanics to data management.

What happens if I forget my Master Password?

PassVault Pro operates on a Zero-Knowledge Architecture. This means we do not store, transmit, or have any way to access your Master Password. If you forget it, there is absolutely no way to recover your vault. Please ensure your Master Password is memorable but highly secure.

Is my data stored in the cloud?

No. By design, PassVault Pro is a 100% offline, local-first application. Your encrypted passwords remain exclusively on the device where you created them. No servers, no databases, and no cloud leaks.

How do I backup or transfer my vault to another device?

You can securely export your vault from the dashboard. This generates an encrypted JSON or CSV file that you can safely transfer to your new device (e.g., via a USB drive). On the new device, simply use the "Import" feature inside the app to restore your vault.

Does the 4-digit PIN replace the Master Password?

The PIN is a convenience feature for quick unlocking when you are actively using the device. However, the PIN is cryptographically tied to your Master Password. If you clear your browser data or log out completely, you will need your full Master Password to log back in.

How do I install it on my phone?

PassVault Pro is built as a Progressive Web App (PWA). Simply open the application in Safari (iOS) or Chrome (Android), tap the "Share" or "Menu" icon, and select "Add to Home Screen". It will function identically to a native app, complete with offline capabilities.

How does the Security Health Audit work?

The audit continuously runs completely locally within your browser. It calculates entropy for password strength, checks for exact string matches to flag reused passwords, and warns you if passwords are becoming old, ensuring your vault maintains a perfect security score.

What encryption standard is used?

We utilize the native browser Web Crypto API. Your master password is hashed using SHA-256 before being utilized as an encryption key. Because it relies on native browser cryptography, it is incredibly fast and adheres to modern military-grade security standards.
